# Contributor License Agreement (CLA)

Mnemos uses a **Contributor License Agreement** so the project can ship
under a permissive open-source license today while preserving the option
to dual-license (e.g. an Enterprise edition) in the future, without
having to track down every prior contributor for permission.

## How signing works

When you open your first pull request to this repository, a bot
(cla-assistant.io) will post a comment with a sign-in link. Click it,
authenticate with GitHub, and the bot records your acceptance for all
future PRs you submit to any `cosmicflow-space` repository. You only
sign once.

You do not need to sign in advance — just open the PR and the bot will
prompt you.

## What you agree to

By signing, you confirm (in summary):

1. **You wrote it or have the right to contribute it.** Either the code is
   your original work, or you have permission from the actual author/owner
   to contribute it under the project's license.
2. **You grant Zen Algorithms LLC a license to use your contribution.** This is
   a non-exclusive, worldwide, royalty-free copyright and patent license
   covering your contribution. You keep ownership of your work — you're
   licensing it, not transferring it.
3. **You permit re-licensing.** Zen Algorithms LLC may distribute your
   contribution under the project's current license (MIT) and under any
   other license the project may adopt in the future (e.g. a commercial
   license for an Enterprise edition).
4. **You disclose conflicts.** If your employment or other agreements give
   anyone else rights over your work, you disclose that or get explicit
   permission first.
5. **No warranty.** You provide the contribution as-is.

## Why this exists

Without a CLA, every contributor independently holds the copyright on
their contribution under MIT. If Mnemos later wants to offer a paid tier
or be acquired, the buyer needs unambiguous rights to commercialize the
whole codebase. Tracking down every past contributor to re-permission
their code is, in practice, impossible.

The CLA fixes this once, at contribution time, while still leaving you
as the copyright holder of your own work.
