# Aurora One — Product Specification

*Portable solar-powered espresso maker. Internal spec, revision C (2026-03).*

## Overview

The **Aurora One** brews a single 40 ml double shot of espresso anywhere there is
sunlight — no mains power, no gas, no batteries to swap. It pairs a foldable
20 W solar panel with a 9-bar piston pump and a 120 ml insulated water chamber.

## Key specifications

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Brew pressure | 9 bar (regulated) |
| Shot volume | 40 ml (double), 20 ml (single) |
| Water chamber | 120 ml, double-wall vacuum insulated |
| Heat-up time (full sun) | 4 minutes to 92 °C |
| Brew time | 28 seconds at target pressure |
| Solar panel | 20 W foldable, monocrystalline |
| Weight (packed) | 690 g |
| Packed dimensions | 180 × 90 × 70 mm |
| Operating temperature | −5 °C to 45 °C |
| Materials | Anodized aluminium, food-grade silicone, Tritan |

## How it works

1. Fill the 120 ml chamber with water.
2. Unfold the solar panel and aim it at the sun. The induction heater reaches
   the 92 °C brew target in about **4 minutes** under full sun.
3. Load 18 g of ground coffee into the portafilter basket.
4. Pump the piston handle to build pressure; the regulator caps output at 9 bar.
5. Press brew. A full double shot pulls in **28 seconds**.

In overcast conditions, heat-up extends to roughly 9 minutes; the field test
report covers cold and low-light performance in detail.

## Care

Rinse the chamber and portafilter after every brew. Descale monthly with the
citric-acid routine described in the support FAQ. The piston seal is a
consumable — see the FAQ for the replacement interval and warranty coverage.
